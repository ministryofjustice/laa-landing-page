Accessibility statement for Sign in to Legal Aid Services

This accessibility statement applies to the - your-legal-aid-services.service.justice.gov.uk. ‘Sign in to Legal Aid Services’ - your-legal-aid-services.service.justice.gov.uk. The service is owned by the Legal Aid Agency (LAA).

We want as many people as possible to be able to use this service. For example, you should be able to:

  • change colours, contrast levels and fonts
  • zoom in up to 400% without the text spilling off the screen
  • navigate most of the service using just a keyboard
  • navigate most of the service using speech recognition software
  • listen to most of the service using a screen reader (including the latest versions of JAWS, NVDA and VoiceOver)

We also try to make the text as simple as possible to understand.

Jump to AbilityNet has advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability.

How accessible this website is

This service is fully compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 AA.

An external accessibility audit previously identified several issues relating to form error handling, focus order, labelling, landmarks and link context. All of these issues have now been fixed.

Feedback and contact information

We’re always looking to improve the accessibility of this service. If you find any problems not listed on this page or think we’re not meeting accessibility requirements, email online-support@justice.gov.uk. When you contact us, please include details of:

  • the page you were using
  • what you were trying to do, and
  • the assistive technology, browser and device you use

If you need information in a different format (like accessible PDF, large print, easy read, audio recording or Braille), email online-support@justice.gov.uk. We’ll consider your request and get back to you within 8 days.

Enforcement procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the ‘accessibility regulations’). If you’re not happy with how we respond to your complaint, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS).

Technical information about this service’s accessibility

The LAA is committed to making its service accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018.

Compliance status

This service is fully compliant with Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.2 AA standard.

Third-party content

This service may direct some users to third-party websites and applications. We do not control these third-party websites and are not responsible for their accessibility compliance.

Preparation of this accessibility statement

This service was audited by User Vision, who tested key user journeys using a mix of manual and automated methods across Windows and MacOS with NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver and Dragon NaturallySpeaking, and browsers including Chrome, Edge, Firefox and Safari. The audit covered the journeys Create a new user, Manage access, Convert to multi‑firm and Delete user.
